# Phase 5 Presentation and Reflection Guide

## Presentation Guidelines
1. **Duration**: Ensure your presentation lasts between 15 to 20 minutes.
2. **Structure**: 
   - **Introduction**: Briefly introduce yourself and the topic. 
   - **Overview**: Outline the main points that will be covered in your presentation. 
   - **Content**: Deliver the detailed content as per the focus of the project. 
   - **Conclusion**: Summarize the key points and offer final thoughts.
3. **Visual Aids**: Use slides or other visual materials to enhance the understanding of your content. Ensure they are clear and not cluttered.
4. **Engagement**: Encourage audience participation by asking questions and facilitating discussions.
5. **Practice**: Rehearse your presentation multiple times to improve delivery and timing.

## Reflection Questions
1. **What did I learn during this phase?**
   - Reflect on the new insights or knowledge gained.
2. **What challenges did I face, and how did I overcome them?**
   - Identify specific obstacles and your approaches to resolving them.
3. **How did I collaborate with my peers?**
   - Discuss the dynamics of teamwork and contributions.
4. **What would I have done differently?**
   - Evaluate your choices and consider alternative approaches.
5. **What is my overall impression of this phase?**
   - Provide a summary of your thoughts regarding this part of the project.

## Self-Evaluation Activities
1. **Rate Your Performance**: Use a scale of 1-10 to assess your contribution and participation.
2. **Peer Feedback**: Ask team members for feedback on your performance and conduct.
3. **List Achievements**: Document the key achievements during this phase and how they contribute to your learning goals.
4. **Create an Action Plan**: Based on reflections, outline steps for improvement in the next phase.
5. **Set New Goals**: Establish specific targets for personal and academic growth moving forward.
